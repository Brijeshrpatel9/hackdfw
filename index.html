February 28 - March 1, 2014

HackDFW is Dallas's first major college hackathon. Hosted by students at UTD, SMU, and UNT, HackDFW brings all of the Dallas area schools together in one exciting 24-hour event.

FAQ

When is HackDFW?

The gates will open on Friday, February 28th and continue until Sunday, March 1, when prizes will be given out. The full schedule will be posted soon.

Who can attend?

All students are welcome! This includes high schoolers, undergraduate students, and graduate students!

How big are teams?

Teams are composed of 2-5 people. You can do it alone, but it'll be less fun!

What if I don't have teammates?

We'll have a time scheduled where people can form teams.

How much does this event cost?

HackDFW is 100% free! This includes WiFi, food, drinks, and miscellaneous swag for the entire hackathon!

What should I bring?

Laptops, chargers, cellphones, and of course passion are helpful at events such as HackDFW. Toiletries and a change of clothes are also useful. Knives, firearms, and other dangerous things are not permitted at the event.

Are we allowed to build on past projects?

A hackathon is an experience to build something new within a set amount of time. Therefore, you cannot work on past projects. Using your own open-source or third party libraries is fine as long as the project is new.

What kind of hardware will you have?

Our friends at MLH will be providing Oculus Rifts, Myo arm-bands, Leap Motions, and Arduinos to hack on for the duration of the event.
